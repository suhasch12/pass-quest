Ch1
The Values and Principles of the UK
Democracy, rule of law, individual liberty, mutual respect
▶
📌The UK is a democracy — citizens vote for elected representatives in free elections.
📌Rule of Law — everyone must obey the law, including the government and the monarch.
📌Individual Liberty — freedom of speech, religion and expression are protected within the law.
📌Mutual Respect and Tolerance — all faiths and beliefs are respected; discrimination is illegal.
❌Forced marriage, FGM and domestic violence are illegal and will be prosecuted.
✅Responsibilities: obey the law, pay taxes, treat others with respect, support your children's education.
Ch2
What is the UK?
Countries, capitals, patron saints, flags, geography
▶
🏴England — Capital: London. Patron saint: St George (23 April). National flower: Rose.
🏴Scotland — Capital: Edinburgh. Patron saint: St Andrew (30 November). National flower: Thistle.
🏴Wales — Capital: Cardiff. Patron saint: St David (1 March). National flower: Daffodil.
🏴Northern Ireland — Capital: Belfast. Patron saint: St Patrick (17 March). National flower: Shamrock.
🇬🇧The Union Flag (Union Jack) combines the crosses of St George, St Andrew and St Patrick. Wales is not included as it was already united with England.
📌The currency is pounds sterling (GBP). The UK does NOT use the Euro.
📌Driving is on the left side of the road — the opposite of most other countries.
Ch3
A Long and Illustrious History
Stone Age to modern times, monarchs, wars, Empire
▶
📅55 BC — Julius Caesar first invaded Britain (unsuccessful). Romans returned in AD 43 under Emperor Claudius.
📅1066 — Norman Conquest. William the Conqueror defeated King Harold at the Battle of Hastings.
📅1215 — King John signed the Magna Carta, limiting royal power for the first time.
👑Henry VIII — broke from the Catholic Church to form the Church of England. Had six wives.
👑Elizabeth I — defeated the Spanish Armada in 1588. Age of Shakespeare and global exploration.
📅1707 — Act of Union united England, Wales and Scotland into Great Britain.
📅1833 — Slavery Abolition Act abolished slavery throughout the British Empire.
📅1914-1918 — First World War. Over 2 million British casualties.
📅1939-1945 — Second World War. Winston Churchill was Prime Minister.
📅1948 — NHS founded. Empire Windrush arrived with Caribbean workers to help rebuild Britain.
📅1969 — Voting age lowered from 21 to 18.
Ch4
A Modern, Thriving Society
Religion, culture, sport, arts, traditions, bank holidays
▶
⛪The official church is the Church of England (Protestant/Anglican). The monarch is its Supreme Governor.
📌About 59% of people in the UK identify as Christian. Islam is the second largest religion.
🎂UK Bank Holidays: New Year's Day, Good Friday, Easter Monday, Early May Bank Holiday, Spring Bank Holiday, Summer Bank Holiday, Christmas Day, Boxing Day.
🔥Guy Fawkes Night (5 November) — commemorates the failed Gunpowder Plot of 1605 to blow up Parliament.
⚽Football was codified in Britain. The FA was founded in 1863. England won the World Cup in 1966.
🎪Wimbledon — the world's oldest tennis tournament, held annually in London since 1877.
📚Famous British writers include Shakespeare, Jane Austen, Charles Dickens, Roald Dahl and J.K. Rowling.
📻The BBC (British Broadcasting Corporation) was founded in 1927 — the world's oldest national broadcaster.
Ch5
The UK Government, the Law and Your Role
Parliament, elections, legal system, rights, responsibilities
▶
🏛Parliament has two chambers: the House of Commons (elected MPs) and the House of Lords (appointed/hereditary).
📌The Prime Minister leads the government — usually the leader of the largest party in the House of Commons.
📌The UK uses First Past the Post elections — the candidate with the most votes in each constituency wins.
📌The monarch is Head of State with a ceremonial role. The King/Queen gives Royal Assent to new laws.
📅Minimum voting age is 18. You must be on the electoral register to vote.
⚖Devolved governments: Scotland has the Scottish Parliament, Wales has the Senedd, Northern Ireland has the Assembly.
⚖Everyone is innocent until proven guilty. The right to a fair trial is guaranteed.
⚖The Supreme Court is the highest court in the UK — hears final appeals in all civil and criminal cases.
📌Jury service — if called, it is a civic duty. A jury consists of 12 members of the public.
📌Non-emergency police: call 101. Emergency services: call 999.
