Chapter 1 — The Values and Principles of the UK

British values, democracy, rule of law, individual liberty, mutual respect

Core British Values

??The UK is a democracy — citizens vote for elected representatives. Everyone has a say in how the country is governed.
??Rule of Law — everyone, including the government, must obey the law. No one is above it.
??Individual Liberty — people are free to live as they choose within the law. Freedom of speech and religion are protected.
??Mutual Respect and Tolerance — different religions and beliefs are respected. Discrimination is not tolerated.
??These values are sometimes summarised as the Fundamental British Values.

Responsibilities of Residents

✅Obey and respect the law. Pay taxes honestly. Treat others with respect regardless of background.
✅Look after yourself and your family. Support your children's education.
✅Register to vote and participate in democracy. Jury service if called.
⛔Forced marriage, female genital mutilation (FGM), and domestic violence are illegal in the UK.

Chapter 2 — What is the UK?

Countries, geography, national symbols, patron saints, flags

The Four Countries

??England — Capital: London. Patron saint: St George (23 April). National flower: Rose.
??Scotland — Capital: Edinburgh. Patron saint: St Andrew (30 November). National flower: Thistle.
??Wales — Capital: Cardiff. Patron saint: St David (1 March). National flower: Daffodil.
??Northern Ireland — Capital: Belfast. Patron saint: St Patrick (17 March). National flower: Shamrock.

The Union Flag (Union Jack)

????Combines the crosses of St George (England), St Andrew (Scotland), and St Patrick (Ireland). Wales is not represented as it was already united with England.
??The national currency is pounds sterling (£). The UK does NOT use the Euro.
??Driving is on the left side of the road — the opposite of most other countries.

Chapter 3 — A Long and Illustrious History

Stone Age to modern times, monarchs, wars, Empire

Early Britain

??Stone Age — First farmers arrived about 6,000 years ago. Stonehenge built as a ceremonial site.
??55 BC — Julius Caesar's first Roman invasion (unsuccessful). Romans successfully invaded in AD 43 under Emperor Claudius.
??1066 — Norman Conquest. William the Conqueror defeated King Harold at the Battle of Hastings.
??1215 — Magna Carta signed by King John — limited the power of the monarch for the first time.

Tudors, Stuarts & Empire

??Henry VIII — broke from the Catholic Church to form the Church of England. Had six wives.
??Elizabeth I — defeated the Spanish Armada in 1588. Age of Shakespeare and exploration.
??1707 — Act of Union united England, Wales and Scotland into the Kingdom of Great Britain.
??1833 — Slavery Abolition Act abolished slavery throughout the British Empire.

20th Century

??1914-1918 — First World War. Over 2 million British casualties.
??1939-1945 — Second World War. Winston Churchill was Prime Minister.
??1948 — NHS founded. Windrush generation arrived from Caribbean to help rebuild Britain.
??1969 — Voting age lowered from 21 to 18.

Chapter 4 — A Modern, Thriving Society

Culture, sport, arts, religion, customs, bank holidays

Religion & Traditions

⛪The official church is the Church of England (Protestant). The monarch is its Supreme Governor.
??About 59% of people identify as Christian. Islam is the second largest religion.
??Bank holidays: New Year's Day, Good Friday, Easter Monday, Early May, Spring, Summer, Christmas Day, Boxing Day.
??Guy Fawkes Night (5 Nov) — commemorates the failed Gunpowder Plot of 1605.

Sport & Culture

⚽Football was developed in Britain. The FA was founded in 1863. England won the World Cup in 1966.
??Wimbledon tennis tournament is the oldest in the world, held annually in London.
??The Grand National (horse racing) and the Ashes (cricket vs Australia) are major sporting events.
??The UK has produced world-famous writers including Shakespeare, Jane Austen, Charles Dickens and Roald Dahl.
??The BBC (British Broadcasting Corporation) was founded in 1927. It is the world's oldest national broadcaster.

Chapter 5 — The UK Government, the Law and Your Role

Parliament, elections, legal system, your rights and responsibilities

Parliament & Government

??Parliament has two chambers: the House of Commons (elected MPs) and the House of Lords (appointed/hereditary peers).
??The Prime Minister is the leader of the government and usually the leader of the largest party in the Commons.
??Elections use First Past the Post — the candidate with the most votes in each constituency wins.
??The monarch is Head of State but has a largely ceremonial role. The King/Queen gives Royal Assent to new laws.
??The minimum voting age is 18. You must be on the electoral register to vote.

The Legal System

⚖️Everyone is innocent until proven guilty. You have the right to a fair trial.
⚖️The Supreme Court is the highest court in the UK. It hears final appeals.
??You must carry your driving licence when driving. The police can ask to see it within 7 days.
??Jury service — if called, it is a civic duty. Juries consist of 12 members of the public.
